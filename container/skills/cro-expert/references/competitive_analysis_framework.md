# Competitive Analysis Framework for Landing Pages

This framework provides a structured way to quickly analyze competitor landing pages to inform your own audit and recommendations.

## Analysis Protocol

For each competitor URL provided:

1.  **Visit the URL** in the browser.
2.  **Spend no more than 3-5 minutes** per competitor. The goal is to identify key patterns, not to perform a full audit.
3.  **Focus on the above-the-fold (hero) section** as this is the most critical area.
4.  **Document your findings** in a simple table or list.

## Key Areas to Analyze

| Element | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| **Headline & Value Prop** | - What is their main promise? 
- How do they differentiate themselves? 
- Is it clear, specific, and benefit-driven? |
| **Primary CTA** | - What is the button copy? (e.g., "Start Free Trial", "Get a Demo", "See Pricing")
- What color is it? Where is it placed? |
| **Social Proof & Trust** | - Do they use customer logos? 
- Are there testimonials? 
- Do they mention customer counts or awards? |
| **Hero Visual** | - Is it a product screenshot, a custom illustration, a stock photo, or a video? |
| **Offer / Model** | - Are they offering a free trial, a demo, a direct purchase, or something else? |
| **Overall Design & Feel** | - Is the design modern and professional, or dated? 
- How does the brand feel (e.g., corporate, startup, playful)? |

## Example Analysis Output

**Competitor A: `competitor.com/features`**
- **Headline**: "The All-in-One Platform for Remote Teams"
- **CTA**: "Start Your 14-Day Free Trial" (Orange)
- **Social Proof**: Logo bar with Slack, Dropbox, Shopify
- **Visual**: Animated product GIF
- **Offer**: 14-day free trial, no credit card required

**Competitor B: `another-competitor.com`**
- **Headline**: "Finally, Project Management That Doesn’t Suck"
- **CTA**: "Request a Demo" (Blue)
- **Social Proof**: Testimonial from a well-known CEO
- **Visual**: Illustration of a calm, organized workspace
- **Offer**: Sales demo

## Synthesizing Findings

After analyzing 3-5 competitors, summarize your findings:

- **Common CTA**: Is everyone pushing a free trial, or are demos more common?
- **Standard Social Proof**: Is a logo bar table stakes in this industry?
- **Dominant Messaging Angle**: Are competitors focusing on features, benefits, or solving a specific pain point?
- **Design Trends**: What is the expected level of design quality in this market?

Use these insights to inform your recommendations. For example, if all competitors offer a free trial and your client doesn't, that's a major strategic gap to highlight.
