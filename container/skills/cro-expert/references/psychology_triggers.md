# CRO Psychology & Behavioral Triggers

This document provides a quick reference for common psychological principles used in conversion rate optimization. Use these to explain the rationale behind your recommendations.

| Principle | Description | Landing Page Application |
|---|---|---|
| **Social Proof** | People are more likely to do something if they see others doing it. | Testimonials, case studies, "as seen on" logos, customer counts, real-time sales notifications. |
| **Authority** | People trust experts and credible sources. | Expert endorsements, industry awards, certifications, data from reputable sources, professional design. |
| **Scarcity / Urgency** | People want what is limited. Scarcity is limited quantity; urgency is limited time. | "Only 3 left in stock!", "Offer ends in 24 hours", countdown timers, limited-edition offers. |
| **Reciprocity** | People feel obligated to give back after receiving something of value. | Free guides, free tools, valuable content, a free consultation. The landing page offer itself is a form of reciprocity. |
| **Commitment & Consistency** | People like to act in ways that are consistent with their previous actions and statements. | Multi-step forms (start with easy questions), free trials that convert to paid, asking for a micro-commitment (like an email) before a larger one (a sale). |
| **Liking** | People are more easily persuaded by people or brands they like. | An "About Us" section with relatable stories, using photos of real team members, a brand voice that resonates with the target audience. |
| **Loss Aversion** | The pain of losing is psychologically twice as powerful as the pleasure of gaining. | Frame offers around what the user will *lose* by not acting. "Stop losing customers," "Don't miss out on...", risk-free guarantees. |
| **Cognitive Ease** | The brain prefers to think as little as possible. Clarity and simplicity are paramount. | Clear headlines, simple language, scannable layouts, minimal form fields, obvious CTAs. This is the foundation of high-converting pages. |
| **Anchoring** | The first piece of information presented affects subsequent judgments. | Show a higher price crossed out next to the current price ("$199" vs "$99"), present the most expensive plan first. |
| **Risk Reversal** | Remove or reduce the perceived risk of taking action. | Money-back guarantees, free trials, "cancel anytime" policies, testimonials from happy customers, secure payment logos. |
